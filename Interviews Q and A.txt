What is Software Testing?
• Software Testing is the process of finding defects to ensure the application meets requirements.

What is SDLC and STLC?

What is the difference between Verification and Validation?
• Verification - Ensures the product is being built correctly (process-oriented).
• Validation: Ensures the right product is built (product-oriented).

What are Functional and Non-functional testing?
• Functional Testing: Validates the business logic and features.
• Non-Functional Testing: Checks performance, usability, and reliability.


What is Regression Testing?

Regression Testing ensures that new changes (code updates, bug fixes, enhancements) have not broken existing
features.
• It’s repeated after every change in the application.
• Often automated for efficiency.

What is Sanity vs Smoke testing?
• Smoke Testing: Broad, shallow testing after a new build. Ensures major functions work.
• Sanity Testing: Narrow, deep testing after a minor fix to check specific functionality.

What is Severity and Priority? Explain with examples.
Severity: Impact of a defect on the system.
Example: A crash in the payment gateway is Critical Severity.
• Priority: Order in which the defect should be fixed.
Example: A spelling mistake on the home page may have low severity but high priority if it impacts branding

How will you report a defect?
When I find a defect, I first reproduce it to confirm. Then I collect evidence like screenshots or logs and check requirements to ensure it’s a valid bug. Next, I log it in Jira with a clear title, detailed steps to reproduce, expected vs actual results, severity/priority, and environment details. I attach all supporting documents. If it’s a critical issue, I immediately inform the developer or lead. Finally, I track the bug until it is fixed and re-tested.”
